Introduction

The mridangam is an ancient percussion instrument originating from the Indian subcontinent.
It is the primary rhythmic accompaniment in a Carnatic music ensemble.
The key components of a mridangam are:

  • Thoppi
  • Mettu
  • Karanai (Soru)

Etymology

The word "Mridangam" is formulated by the union (sandhi) of the two Sanskrit words m≈ód (clay or earth) and anga (limb), as the earliest versions of the instrument were made of hardened clay.
